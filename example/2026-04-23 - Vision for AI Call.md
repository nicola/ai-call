# AI Call — Vision for AI Call
**Date:** 2026-04-23

---

## Q1: What's the core problem you're solving with AI Call?

**Answer:** I find myself chatting a lot with AI, and I realized that talking to the AI for long periods and doing larger brain dumps is useful. It turns out this is also what we do with people — we meet for a brainstorm call, and during the call someone does a brain dump. Ideally anyone should be able to talk in free form and generate all of these notes.

So number one, this is a tool for personal brainstorming. Number two, imagine you want to have a call with someone else and you already know the questions. It's hard for someone to sit down and brainstorm and think for a long time, but if they have an AI prompting them questions, they're more likely to have long-form brainstorm conversations or interviews.

For example, if I want to ask questions to a colleague, I can either ask them directly, or I can send them an AI call — or even schedule time in their calendar to talk to the AI and then give me back the report. At some point the AI might also, as part of a sub-task, be told to ask questions to someone else. One thing is asking some questions, another thing is asking for proper focus time from someone. An AI call is basically a way for people — it's some sort of tool execution where we prompt a human.

---

## Q2: How would the flow of sending someone an AI call actually work, step by step?

**Answer:** I would open the computer, talk to my AI, and write a bullet point list of questions I'd like to ask someone. The AI gives me a better form of these questions, and using the AI call skill, I'd say "generate an AI call file." It generates an AI call markdown file, and then I grab and drop it and send it to someone via email, messaging, and so on.

In the future I think there will be websites that do all of this plus actual scheduling. Once they receive it, they drag and drop the MD file into their preferred tool, or through the AI call website. They can use voice, they can use text. The AI call website is a simple open-source website or app that facilitates all of that. For pro users, they can just drop it into their preferred AI and continue from there.

---

## Q3: Why is an AI asking you questions better at getting a brain dump than just sitting down and writing your thoughts out?

**Answer:** Writing down your thoughts works, but not everyone thinks that way. Sometimes people need to be asked to schedule some time to do the work. Focusing proper time is a way to get ideas out, and not everyone has the self-discipline for that. Plus, AI has reached a level where it can ask great questions. It can ask great follow-up questions to what you just said, and it could prompt ideas that maybe you wouldn't have had otherwise.

---

## Q4: When would an AI decide on its own that it needs to talk to a human?

**Answer:** Imagine you task an AI to do some work like making a website. The AI starts working, and at some point it realizes it needs to ask questions to the user. If the AI has the AI call skill, it could just stop and ask for human-in-the-loop, running this AI call interaction. Otherwise, if the AI is smarter and doesn't need the answer urgently, and it has access to the person's calendar, it could put some time in their calendar that says "I want you to go over this set of questions — drag and drop this file to your AI to start an AI call."

---

## Q5: What do people do with the results of an AI call?

**Answer:** An AI call generates transcripts of a conversation, and this can become part of the context of someone else's work. For example, I ask five reportees some questions, then I get their transcript answers, and I can ask the AI about what they replied without me necessarily going through all the documents. You can also ask the AI to turn them into documentation, summaries, and so on.

### Follow-up: So the transcript becomes an input to other AI workflows?

**Answer:** Exactly. The transcript is just a markdown file, so it fits naturally into any AI context. You can feed five transcripts into an AI and say "summarize the common themes" or "draft a report based on these answers." It closes the loop — you send out AI calls, get structured responses back, and then use AI again to synthesize everything.

---

## Q6: Where do you see AI Call in a year or two — is it a feature, a product, or a protocol?

**Answer:** I think AI call is just an idea. People can implement it in whatever way they want. I'm implementing this as a skill for Pi because I use Pi, but anyone can install this skill on Claude, on Codex, or whatever AI tool they want. I do plan to make an app or a website for those that aren't pro users, so they can just have a call with AI without installing anything. But ideally I'd like to do that in a way that is private — data don't go to me — because I think a lot of these calls are meant to be private. I do think this might turn into a product, but I also think there will be an explosion of AI call software.

---

## Summary

AI Call is envisioned as a portable, open format for structured human-AI conversations — starting as a brainstorming tool for individuals, but expanding into an asynchronous collaboration protocol where people can send AI-guided interviews to colleagues, schedule them on calendars, and even have AI agents proactively request focused human input as part of larger tasks. The core insight is that AI calls are "tool execution where we prompt a human" — inverting the typical AI interaction model. The project prioritizes openness (anyone can implement it), portability (it's just a markdown file), and privacy (data stays with the user).
